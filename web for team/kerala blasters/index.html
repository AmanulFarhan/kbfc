History

Kerala Blasters FC is a professional football club based in Kochi, Kerala, India. The club was founded in 2014 as part of the inaugural season of the Indian Super League (ISL), which is the top professional football league in India. The team is owned by former Indian cricket captain Sachin Tendulkar, along with a consortium of investors.
Here is a brief history of Kerala Blasters FC:
1. Formation (2014): Kerala Blasters FC was one of the eight franchises that participated in the first season of the Indian Super League, which kicked off in 2014. The team quickly gained popularity due to its strong fan base and vibrant atmosphere at home matches.
2. Inaugural Season (2014): In their debut season, Kerala Blasters reached the ISL final under the guidance of English coach David James. The team faced Atlético de Kolkata in the final but narrowly lost in a penalty shootout.
3. ISL 2015 and 2016: In the subsequent seasons, Kerala Blasters had mixed performances and did not reach the heights of their inaugural season. However, the fan support remained strong, and the team continued to be one of the most followed clubs in the league.
4. Title Success in 2016: The 2016 season saw Kerala Blasters reach the ISL final once again, this time under the coaching of Steve Coppell. In a thrilling final, they faced Atlético de Kolkata once more and emerged victorious, winning the championship with a 4-3 aggregate score.
5. Subsequent Seasons: Kerala Blasters had varying degrees of success in the following seasons, with changes in coaching staff and player rosters. Despite not securing another championship, the team consistently maintained a strong fan base and remained a popular entity in Indian football.
6. Recent Years: The subsequent seasons saw Kerala Blasters continuing to be a competitive team in the league, and they consistently attracted large crowds to their home matches. The club has been known for its passionate fan base, known as the "Manjappada," who create a vibrant atmosphere in the stadium.
Kerala Blasters FC has become a significant and beloved football club in India, with a fan base that passionately supports the team through thick and thin. The club's history is still evolving, and they continue to be an integral part of the Indian Super League.

Jawaharlal Nehru International Stadium, commonly known as Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium or JLN Stadium, is a multipurpose stadium located in Kochi, Kerala, India. The stadium is named after Jawaharlal Nehru, the first Prime Minister of India. It serves as one of the prominent sports venues in the region and has hosted various national and international events. Here are some key features and information about Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium, Kochi:
1. Capacity: The stadium has a seating capacity of around 39,000 spectators, making it one of the largest stadiums in India.
2. Facilities: Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium is equipped with modern facilities, including a synthetic track, football and athletics fields, and facilities for other sports. It meets the standards required for hosting international athletic competitions.
3. Football: The stadium is primarily known as a football venue and has been a key host for football matches, including Indian Super League (ISL) matches. Kerala Blasters FC, one of the prominent teams in the ISL, uses Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium as its home ground for home matches.
4. Events: Apart from football, the stadium has hosted various other events, including cultural programs, music concerts, and international sports events. It has been a venue for major tournaments, helping promote sports and entertainment in the region.
5. Renovation: The stadium underwent significant renovations and upgrades in preparation for the 2017 FIFA U-17 World Cup, which India hosted. These renovations included improvements to the seating, facilities, and overall infrastructure to meet the standards of an international sporting event.
Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium in Kochi plays a crucial role in promoting sports and hosting various events, contributing to the cultural and sporting landscape of the region. It is a significant venue for football enthusiasts and has witnessed memorable moments in the sport.
